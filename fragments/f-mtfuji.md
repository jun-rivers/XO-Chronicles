Fragment: Mt. Fuji

Mount Fuji, at 3,776 metres, is Japan's highest peak and an active stratovolcano last erupted in 1707. For centuries it has been a site of Shinto pilgrimage, its summit home to the Okumiya shrine of Fujisan Hongū Sengen Taisha. Approximately 300,000 people climbed it annually in the early 21st century.

_In MF45, the climbing trails are buried under volcanic ash deposits from the MF12 eruption. The summit shrine still stands. Two monks maintain it. Most days, no one comes._
