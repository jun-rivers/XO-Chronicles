Fragment: Batteries

Lithium-ion batteries, first commercialized in 1991, became the foundational energy storage technology of the 21st century. At peak production, global battery manufacturing consumed more cobalt than any other industry, most of it extracted from the Democratic Republic of Congo.

_By MF20, the scarcity of cobalt had made functional batteries a primary unit of trade across Nomad settlements. A battery pack capable of powering a climate suit for 30 days was worth, in most desert markets, more than a human life. This comparison was not considered impolite._
